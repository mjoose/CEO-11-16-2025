We have prepared.

Most of what goes wrong on a renovation goes wrong because something wasn’t decided early enough. A scope is vague. A long-lead item is discovered too late. A decision gets made in a hallway and disappears.

Adpar is built to close those gaps before they open. The documents and cadence on this site are the same ones we use on real projects. The proof is not in marketing—it’s in operational visibility.

What we deliver

Artifacts that keep a renovation honest

Line-item estimate
A scope you can actually review: assemblies, allowances, and the decisions required to price work honestly.
CSI scope of work
Trade-by-trade scope tied to drawings and specifications, written so nothing disappears between disciplines.
Procurement log
Long-lead items tracked early—submittals, lead times, and delivery dates visible before they become schedule risk.
Weekly site report
A concise record of progress, decisions, photos, and open items—built for architects who want clarity, not noise.
QA / punch discipline
Quality checks that happen during work, not at the end—so closeout feels earned, not negotiated.
